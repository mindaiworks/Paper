# The Eternal Path of AGI

## Introduction
From induction to deduction, toward true wisdom  

In recent years, large language models (LLMs) have rapidly developed, demonstrating astonishing generative capabilities.  
They can write articles, draw pictures, translate, program, and even simulate human conversation.  

However, while people marvel at their abilities, a core issue has emerged:  
LLMs excel at **induction**, but are not truly adept at **deduction**.  

This distinction marks the critical boundary between current AI and true AGI (Artificial General Intelligence).  

If induction and deduction are likened to yin and yang in Taiji:  
- Induction is yin  
- Deduction is yang  

True AGI must be a wisdom system where yin and yang are unified.

## The Essence of Large Models: Induction

### What is Induction?
Induction is the method of finding common patterns from numerous observations.  

Example:  
- Apples fall down  
- Bananas fall down  
- Oranges fall down  

Inductive result: Objects fall downward.  
Core of induction: deriving common rules from multiple outcomes.

### The Nature of LLMs
Large language models are essentially **massive statistical induction systems**.  

Their abilities come from:  
- Massive text  
- Massive images  
- Massive knowledge data  

Training builds co-occurrence relationships:  
- Word → Word  
- Sentence → Sentence  
- Image → Image  
- Event → Event  

Examples:  
- Doctor ↔ Hospital  
- Rain ↔ Umbrella  
- Program ↔ Computer  

These are associations, not causality.

### Advantages of Induction
- **Strong generative ability**: quickly create articles, images, music, code  
- **Strong pattern recognition**: discover hidden rules, user preferences, market trends  
- **Strong knowledge compression**: condense billions of data points into model parameters  

---

## Limitations of Induction

### Association ≠ Causation
Example:  
Ice cream sales increase ↔ Drowning incidents increase.  
They are correlated, but the true cause is summer heat.  

Looking only at correlation may misjudge “ice cream causes drowning.”  
This is the trap of induction.

### Source of AI Hallucinations
AI hallucination is essentially: **statistically plausible but logically wrong**.  
Models may generate nonexistent papers, people, or incorrect citations because they predict the most likely answer, not verify its truth.

## High-Risk Domains: Induction Alone is Insufficient

| Domain | What Induction Can Do | Where Induction Fails |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Medicine | Symptom patterns | Cannot find root cause |
| Finance | Market patterns | Cannot find economic causality |
| Industrial Safety | Accident statistics | Cannot find root cause |
| Law | Case statistics | Cannot determine legal responsibility |

### Medicine
Induction finds symptom correlations and disease probabilities but confuses correlation with causation.  
Needs: **Induction + Pathology + Causal reasoning**

### Finance
Induction finds market patterns and historical rules but ignores policy and behavior factors.  
Needs: **Induction + Economic models + Game theory + Causal reasoning**

### Industrial Safety
Induction finds accident statistics but not root causes.  
Needs: **Induction + Fault analysis + Root cause analysis + Causal reasoning**

### Law
Induction analyzes past judgments and similar cases, but law is based on deduction.  
Example:  
All intentional killers should be punished → Zhang committed intentional killing → Zhang should be punished.  

Law also requires causal and normative reasoning:  
Fact existence ≠ legality ≠ guilt.  
Needs: **Facts + Statutes + Judicial interpretation + Precedents + Logical reasoning**

## The Way of Taiji: Yin-Yang Complementarity

### Yin: Induction
Like the ocean.  
Features: inclusive, absorbs information, discovers patterns.  
Strengths: generation, learning, association.

### Yang: Deduction
Like the sword.  
Features: precise, rigorous, verifiable.  
Strengths: reasoning, validation, decision-making, design.

### Yin-Yang Interdependence
- Induction alone: knowledge without guarantee  
- Deduction alone: logic without knowledge  
- Together: knowledge + logic → true wisdom
